The Reputation and
Effects of MSG
What is MSG
MSG or monosodium glutamate is a known food additive and flavour enhancer, commonly linked to Chinese and Japanese cuisine. It is naturally found in small amounts in some foods such as tomato and cheese, but more known as a flavour enhancer utilised in Chinese cuisine to provide a distinct taste.

The Origins and
Beginnings in America
MSG was first discovered in 1907 by Japanese chemist Kikunae Ikeda, from foods like Tomato and Asparagus and extracting the glutamate with a mixture of water and salt. By the 1950s it was being sold in America in bottles, where it was heavily associated with Chinese cuisine.
In 1968, the New England Journal of Medicine published a letter from a doctor whocomplained about muscle pain, particularly after eating Chinese Cuisine. He suggested that MSG, cooking wine or excessive salt were to blame, similar responses were recorded from readings and therefore a common blame towards MSG was born. This caused MSG to become a bit of a scapegoat to these issues, creating the 'Chinese Restaurant Syndrome' where many who were not used to the food felt the effects. Early on, researchers blamed MSG for the similar symptoms that the doctor experienced, even when there were flaws to the claim as many who consumed it without knowledge often did not experience the symptoms.
By 1995, the FDA (Food and Drug Administration) released a report from the Federation of American Societies for Experimental Biology which stated that MSG is safe for consumption at small quantities which was similar to that used in Chinese Cuisine. This however did not necessarily change the story, where many still felt that MSG was the culprit for the negative effects and still succumbed to the Restaurant Syndrome.
What contributed to its
current perception
1.
MSG was relatively unknown to America and therefore had no concrete evidence of positive or negative effects on a person
2.
It was associated with Chinese cuisine, which can arguably link to racial prejudice as the negative reputation developed in America
3.
Papers, articles and other forms of physical writings condemned MSG as a negative substance due to personal experience of the writers which inluded doctors and other experts
The rumours of MSG
The information surrounding MSG could also be attributed to the mistrust towards Chinese cuisine and its people, as there could definitely be a connection between racial prejudice and the theory. It could also be considered that a new substance to a country can be often blamed for any problems, as MSG had little information about its health effects at the time, leading to theories and fear to be spread about its attributes. The Chinese Restaurant Syndrome can definitely be linked to any racial bias America had at the time, seeing certain food as different and blaming a newer substance for its negative effects.
Papers and articles from trusted figures certainly reinforced the negative rumours surrounding MSG, where many complained and blamed MSG, it caused many to share the same opinion. It also did not help that experts like doctors condemned the compound, as opposed to researching and finding the real reason for these symptoms within Chinese cuisine. Therefore people often blamed the unknown compound of MSG. The compound of MSG did not have that much research put into it at that time, making it seem untrustworthy in comparison to known flavour enhancers.
What does MSG do to us?
The supposed MSG side effects are often found with no real link to the consumption of MSG. In the 1990s, The FDA asked the Federation of American Societies for Experimental Biology to look into the effects and overall safety of MSG. They noted that there were little effects and any effects were very mild and short-lived, and these were typically associated with large doses of MSG without any food. In saying that it could be common for those who are not used to the substance to have some type of reaction, similar to if you were to consume a new food after years without consumption. Therefore small amounts of MSG will not likely have any negative side effects.
